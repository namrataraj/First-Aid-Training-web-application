Poisoning First Aid

Why This Matters

Poisoning occurs when a person ingests, inhales, or comes into contact with toxic substances. Quick and appropriate action can prevent serious complications or even save a life. Knowing how to recognize poisoning and provide proper first aid is essential, especially in households with children or in workplaces with hazardous materials.

1
Signs and Symptoms of Poisoning
Respiratory Symptoms
  • • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • • Chest pain or tightness
Digestive Symptoms
  • • Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
  • • Excessive salivation or drooling
Neurological Symptoms
  • • Drowsiness, confusion, or dizziness
  • • Seizures
  • • Abnormal pupil size (dilated or constricted)
Skin Symptoms
  • • Burns or redness around the mouth or skin (if the poison is corrosive)
  • • Rashes or irritation
Important Note

Symptoms can vary widely depending on the type of poison, amount, and how it entered the body. Some poisons may not cause immediate symptoms. When in doubt, always seek medical help.

2
First Aid for Different Types of Poisoning

The appropriate first aid depends on how the poison entered the body:

1. Ingestion Poisoning
  • Call for help via the emergency helpline number
  • DO NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by a medical professional
  • If the person is conscious, offer them water or milk (only if instructed by a medical professional)
  • Identify the poison if possible (bring the container, plant, or substance involved)
Never try to neutralize a poison with lemon juice, vinegar, or other substances unless directed by poison control.
2. Inhalation Poisoning
  • Get the person to fresh air immediately while keeping yourself safe from exposure
  • If unconscious but breathing, place them in recovery position
  • Call emergency services immediately
Common inhalation poisons include carbon monoxide, chlorine gas, and chemical fumes. Always ensure your own safety first.
3. Contact Poisoning
  • Wash the affected skin immediately with soap and water
  • If caused by a plant (e.g., poison ivy), wash the area and avoid scratching
  • For chemical burns, flush the area with lots of water for at least 20 minutes
  • Remove contaminated clothing using gloves
  • Seek medical attention if severe reactions occur
4. Injection Poisoning
  • Call emergency services immediately
  • If the substance is known (e.g., heroin), administer Naloxone (Narcan) if available
  • For stings or bites, remove the stinger or needle carefully without squeezing
  • Monitor breathing and pulse, be ready to perform CPR
Injection poisoning includes drug overdoses, snake bites, and insect stings. Time is critical in these cases.
3
When to Seek Medical Help
Unconsciousness

If the person is unconscious, drowsy, or confused, call emergency services immediately.

Breathing Difficulty

If the person is showing difficulty breathing or is experiencing chest pain, seek immediate medical attention.

Severe Symptoms

If the person shows signs of severe poisoning (e.g., seizures, paralysis, severe burns), call emergency services immediately.

Vulnerable Individuals

If the poisoning involves a child or elderly person, seek medical help even for minor symptoms.

Critical Warning

If you cannot identify the poison or are unsure about what to do, always err on the side of caution and seek professional medical care or call poison control. Time is critical in poisoning cases.
