Burns First Aid

Why This Matters

Burns are common injuries that can happen to anyone, anywhere. Knowing how to properly identify and treat burns can prevent complications and may even save a life. Let's learn the essential skills to handle burn emergencies with confidence!

1
Types of Burns
Thermal Burns

Caused by heat sources like fire, hot objects, steam, or scalding liquids. Common in cooking accidents or from hot water.

Cold Burns (Frostbite)

Caused by exposure to freezing temperatures. Often affects outdoor workers, adventurers, or those in extremely cold conditions.

2
Burn Severity
First-Degree Burns (Superficial)
  • Mild discomfort, red and slightly inflamed skin
  • Heals within 7-10 days
  • Seek medical help if the burn is large or in a sensitive area
Second-Degree Burns (Partial Thickness)
  • Red, white, or splotchy skin with blisters
  • Moderate to severe pain
  • A hospital visit is recommended if pain is severe or if the burn is in a sensitive area
Third-Degree Burns (Full Thickness)
  • White, brown, or blackened skin with a leathery texture
  • Often numb due to nerve damage
  • Immediate medical attention is critical!
3
Treatment Steps
1
Cool the Burn

Run cool (not cold) water over the area for 10-15 minutes to reduce pain and swelling.

Never use ice, as it can cause further damage to the skin adn nerves.
2
Protect the Burn

Cover with a clean, non-stick bandage or cloth. Do not use adhesive bandages directly on the burn.

3
Relieve Pain

Over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen can help reduce pain and inflammation.
